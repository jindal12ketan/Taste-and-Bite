North Indian Bhojan

North Indian food is a diverse and flavorful cuisine that includes dishes from various regions of North India, such as Punjab, Uttar Pradesh, Rajasthan, and Kashmir. The cuisine is known for its use of aromatic spices, herbs, and vegetables, which give the dishes their distinct flavor and fragrance.







North Indian cuisine is often characterized by its rich and creamy gravies, which are made by blending tomatoes, onions, and a variety of spices. Many dishes are also cooked in a tandoor oven, which gives them a smoky and charred flavor. Naan bread, which is typically brushed with butter or ghee, is a popular accompaniment to many North Indian dishes.

Delhi Delight

Delhi Shakh

Delhi is renowned for its diverse and flavorful vegetarian cuisine, which reflects the city's rich cultural heritage. The city's vegetarian cuisine is known for its use of aromatic spices and flavorful ingredients, including paneer (Indian cottage cheese), chickpeas, potatoes, and a variety of vegetables.

Delhi Ki Gaalian

Delhi street food is a vibrant and integral part of the city's food culture. The city is famous for its street food, which ranges from savory snacks to sweet desserts. Walking through the narrow streets of Old Delhi, you can find a wide variety of street food vendors selling delicious and affordable snacks.
